steps:
  - text: Rinse the basmati under cold water, swirling, until the water runs nearly clear. Drain well.
    why: Rinsing strips surface starch so the grains steam separate instead of clumping.
  - text: Warm the butter in the pot and toast the cardamom, star anise, and bay leaf 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
    why: Blooming the whole spices in fat pulls their aromatics out and perfumes the whole pot.
  - text: Add the drained rice and stir to coat for about a minute, then add the water and salt.
    why: Coating each grain in the spiced fat seals it slightly and carries the aroma into the rice.
  - text: Bring to a boil, then drop to the lowest heat, lid on, 12–15 minutes. Don't lift the lid.
    why: Trapped steam cooks the rice evenly; peeking releases it and stalls the cook.
  - text: Off the heat, lid on, rest 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork. Fish out the star anise and pods before serving.
    why: The off-heat rest firms the grains and lets residual steam finish the top layer.

Pairs with anything smoke-forward — pulls double duty under grilled chicken or alongside a braise. Warn the table about the whole spices, or pick them out so no one bites a star anise.
