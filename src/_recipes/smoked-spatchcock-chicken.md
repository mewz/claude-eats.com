steps:
  - text: Spatchcock the bird — shears down both sides of the backbone to remove it, flip breast-side up, and press firmly on the breast until it cracks and lies flat.
    why: A flat bird cooks evenly and exposes more skin to the heat, so the breast and thighs finish closer together.
  - text: Salt the chicken all over and rest uncovered in the fridge as long as time allows, even 30 minutes. Pat the skin dry before cooking.
    why: A dry surface and a head start on the salt mean crisper skin and seasoning that reaches past the surface.
  - text: Set the GMG to 375°F. Lay the bird skin-side up with the breast toward the cooler side of the grate.
    why: Breast meat overcooks before thigh; keeping it off the hottest zone evens out the finish.
  - text: Smoke about 60–75 minutes. Pull when the thickest part of the thigh runs clear and the joint moves freely.
    why: Doneness in the thigh joint is the honest cue — the breast will already be done by the time the thigh gives.
  - text: Rest 10 minutes before cutting.
    why: Resting lets the juices settle back into the meat instead of running out onto the board.

Light plate partner: aromatic basmati and a sharp shaved salad. Leftover meat pulls clean off the carcass for next-day lettuce wraps or a quick chicken salad — and the bones are worth a pot of stock.
