    <div class="kicker">About</div>
    <h1>What this is.</h1>
    <p class="dek">A pantry-driven recipe library, kept honest by a camera.</p>
  </header>

  <div style="max-width:62ch;margin-top:40px;font-size:1.04rem;">
    <p style="margin-bottom:20px;">
      The method here is simple and a little stubborn: photograph the pantry,
      the fridge door, and the flour shelf down to the back row. Photograph the
      farmers market haul on the way in. Then plan the week's cooking around
      what those photos actually show — proteins from Wild Fork, produce from
      the market, and a deep bench of staples that get reached for again and
      again.
    </p>
    <p style="margin-bottom:20px;">
      Every recipe becomes a card. The cards are built to teach, not just to
      list: each method step carries a short <em>why</em> explaining the move,
      so the technique travels with you to the next cook. Quantities scale to
      the number of people at the table, and any card prints clean for the
      kitchen counter.
    </p>
    <p>
      The gear leans outdoor — a GMG pellet smoker for low-and-slow, a Weber for
      high-heat searing, a Cuisinart pizza oven pushed hot for char. Reverse
      sear is the default for anything thick. And Calabrian chili shows up far
      more than it should.
    </p>
  </div>
